Initial Problem Discovery

After landing in Madison, WI, as an international student, the first thing to do was to order some groceries from a reputed store. After spending an hour on the Trader Joe's website, I came across the information that they do not sell products online. This cumbersome user experience left me pondering the potential for improvement. I thought there must surely be a solution to bridge this gap and enhance the grocery shopping experience for newcomers like myself.

Problem

A majority of potential shoppers meet a dead end on the Trader Joe's website while trying to checkout items. Other instore shoppers are not aware of the website and hence the user engagement on the website turns out to be quite low.

Why is there a disconnect between Trader Joe's platform and other platforms in the same business domain

Objective

The primary aim of our comprehensive research endeavor was driven by a commitment to understanding and addressing user pain-points on the Trader Joe's website with the overarching goal of enriching the online shopping journey and fostering greater satisfaction among customers.

White Paper Research

We studied the industrial trends and data and found that the trend of combining in-store and online grocery shopping is expected to continue due to the convenience it provides to customers. Also, in response to the post-pandemic grocery trends, enhancing the user experience through the website could be a potential approach. Below are few insights from McKinsey & Company reports:



  • According to McKinsey, online grocery spending is expected to increase to 7.2% in 2023, with a projected market penetration rate of 14-18% in the next 3-5 years.

  • 22% shoppers plan an in-store shopping trip combined with a prior online order, according to Nielsen IQ's research.

Importance of online interface features:
  • Ease of use: product selection, saved preferences, and order history for reordering.


  • Self-serve access to information: stockouts, progress updates, and changes to orders.

Competitor Analysis

We started off with exploring and understanding two leading grocery firms, H-E-B and Kroger to find what factors impacted their success and how could we improve and reflect that in our own problem space.

Our findings revealed, although implementing delivery services might be challenging, a great amount of competitors demonstrate good examples of how they work and how they are benefited from optimizing the user experiences on their websites.

How do our competitors work?

  • H-E-B Express is a fast delivery service that promises to deliver grocery essentials in two hours or less.

  • Kroger provides both delivery and ship services. While delivery service is mainly for weekly groceries, ship service is mainly for heavy items.

Research Methodology: Mixed Method

After our white paper research and competitor analysis, we wanted the first hand target audience experiences to cater to their needs and understand their pain points.

For this, we used mixed method of user interview, contextual inquiry and survey.

Who was our target population?

  • Who have either visited the Trader Joe's website or shopped at a physical Trader Joe's store at least once.
  • Who regularly engage in grocery shopping, both online and offline.
  • Who conduct grocery shopping at least once a month, regardless the location.

User Interviews

Participants: 9

We utilized semi-structured, planned, and one-on-one interviews as our initial research methods to build a basic understanding of individuals' shopping habits. We focused on the participants shopping experience at Trader Joe's store and studied their interactions and expectations of the website.

  • How would you describe your most recent shopping experience?
  • What are the criteria that make your shopping experience an ideal one?
  • In what scenarios have you used Trader Joe's website?
  • How often do you see yourself using Trader Joe's website?
  • When you are on the Trader Joe's website are there any challenges you faced while accessing information?

Contextual Inquiry

Participants: 6

We conducted contextual inquiries on our participants and observed them while they shopped in-store and navigated through the Trader Joe's website to study their overall behaviors and experiences. This was combined with asking open-ended questions and performing think-aloud. Their user stories, successes and breakdowns were documented to be further leveraged for the downstream study. We ensured to have obtained informed consent from all participants for collecting their data. The observation methods used:

  • Observe/ Ask what the participants used do before the visit (e.g., making shopping list, visit website)
  • Physical visit (Think aloud: Understand customers’s thoughts while making decisions with follow-up questions)
  • Invite interviewees to surf the website of Trader Joe’s after a physical store visit

Survey

Participants: Over 25

We conducted a survey to determine the key priorities of our users and assist us in establishing a prioritization strategy. Particularly answering this question: What aspects do customers find important regarding the website of a grocery store and how focusing on enhancing those areas can benefit the business of Trader Joe’. The survey was crafted on Qualtrics.
The survey can be found here.

  • The survey questions were framed keeping in mind the insights gained from interviews and contextul inquiries.
  • We focussed on gathering insights from individuals who have practical experience with grocery shopping, particularly at Trader Joe's.
  • Each one of us recruited a minimum of five respondents to ensure a diverse and representative sample.
  • Furthermore, we extended our reach by sharing the survey link on social media and chat apps.

Processing and Analyzing the data

With all the insights, we organized the data through affinity diagram and tried to find like trends and themes together.

After interviewing 9 participants and survey responses, we brainstormed 140+ data points and came up with 3 themes around user demographics, shopping habits and interactions with Trader Joe's website. They prompted us to below user needs:

  • Clear communication and regarding restricted services offered online.
  • Convenient way to buy their desired items in minimal time.
  • Collaboration on product information exchange because to ensure they have made better choices.
  • Personalized way to navigate products.
  • Efficient way to find information about making a cost-effective purchase on the website.

User Persona

After gaining all the insights, we created our persona:

Meet Anna Chan, our user persona, whose problems and shopping habits closely align with our user group based on budget limitations and preferences for shopping at Trader Joe's.

User Journey Map

We created the user journey based on Anna Chan's persona, considering that the majority of research participants align with her characteristics (limited budget and doesn't prefer Trader Joe's website). We found out that the user faces the most challenge in reaching the store and through product navigation and selection.

Reflections

Overall, this experience has been a game-changer. It's not just about ticking off an assignment; it's about gaining real-world skills and insights that I can carry forward in my career. Can't wait to see where this journey takes me next!
