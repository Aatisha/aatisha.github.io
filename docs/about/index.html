Background

Hi, I'm Aatisha Cyrill, a Master's student in Information Science with a UX concentration at the University of Wisconsin, Madison. I currently serve as the UX and Research Data Initiatives Assistant at the University's Digital Libraries, where I'm redesigning the experience of their open-access institutional repository for research work. Previously, I interned with the Space Science and Engineering Center at UW-Madison, where I had the opportunity to design and build innovative products that support scientific research.

Before pursuing UX, I worked as a Software Engineer specializing in data engineering and analysis. During that time, I realized how design gives meaning and context to the tools we create. This realization motivated me to shift into UX, where I leverage my technical expertise and empathy to craft thoughtful digital experiences backed up by data-informed decisions which truly impact lives.

Every day, I strive to learn, reflect, and create designs that resonate on a human level.

My Inspiration to Design

My passion for design stems from a deep sense of empathy, cultivated through my love of reading. I have been an avid reader and love reading mostly fiction. I find the process of consuming a tale very similar to understanding people and then designing efficient user experiences for them. Reading lets me step into a world where characters grapple with life's deepest questions, dilemmas and emotions, similarly user interviews, journey maps and personas let me experience user knowledge. From both reading and design, I've learned to absorb not just content but the feelings and stories that drive meaningful interactions. And the design process challenges me to think, empathize and contribute in ways to solve real-world problems while creating meaningful and impactful user experiences.
