Design challenge and responsibilities overview

Challenge

Integrate UX principles to develop a platform for Sustain Dane, a sustainability organization serving Dane County, dedicated to fostering environmental consciousness.

Opportunity

Design or reimagine a mobile app for Sustain Dane to encourage users to make sustainable life choices, through collaborative goal setting and environmental awareness.

Background

This project was undertaken within the Information Science program at the University of Wisconsin, Madison, as part of the User Experience Design (LIS 612) Spring course.

Problem

Our planet is getting alarmingly hot, if not now then when?

Climate change is an urgent global challenge that demands our collective attention. As we witness its profound impacts on our planet, it's clear that action is needed at every level - from world leaders to individual citizens.

Reading articles about climate change and watching the news makes people wonder what they can do about it. Because not everyone has the same resources, opportunities, and abilities to make contributions, the cause gets forgotten and unattended. But the fact that the majority of the population uses digital platforms in their daily lives, the platform itself could be leveraged to encourage people to make simple yet effective contributions.

Solution

Empowering Sustainable Choices through Fun, Accountability, and Achievement

To foster a sustainable lifestyle, this app design makes sustainable choices engaging, social, and rewarding. By integrating education, goal-setting, and a recognition system, users are supported at each stage of their sustainability journey.

Environmental Awareness Hub

  • Curated sections like Sustainability Stories, Recycle Better, and Air Quality Map make sustainable information accessible.
  • Users can explore real-life sustainability achievements, fostering inspiration and connection.
  • Share personal stories, sort by relevance, and follow content to stay updated and motivated.

Goal setting individually or with a partner

  • Allows users to set achievable and meaningful goals that fit their lifestyle, promoting gradual, lasting changes.
  • Encourages users to team up with friends or community members, enhancing motivation and commitment through shared goals.
  • Offers flexibility for users to set recurring goals or one-time actions, with the added encouragement of social accountability.

Celebrating achievements and tree planting

  • Upon reaching sustainability milestones, users are rewarded with the opportunity to plant trees.
  • Turn digital accomplishments into tangible environmental contributions.
  • Users receive a personalized certificate recognizing their efforts.

Research

Desk Research

Research shows a significant gap between people's willingness to support environmental conservation and their ability to take consistent, meaningful action (Yale publication, 2024). While individuals express concern for climate change, various barriers prevent them from translating this motivation into tangible, eco-friendly behaviors.

Research

Secondary Research Insights

To further explore these barriers, secondary research included reviewing studies on environmental behavior and user experience (UX) design. A key insight from this research is the multifaceted nature of the obstacles to climate action, which includes psychological, social, and structural/logistical factors.

For instance, studies reveal that for political actions, such as contacting government officials to address climate concerns, the most common barrier for registered voters is that no one has ever asked them to do it. Other common reasons include feeling that their action would make no difference, not considering themselves activists, lacking knowledge of who to contact, and uncertainty about what to say.

Hence without the right support, many users struggle to take meaningful steps. Major barriers to engagement include lack of:

  • Resources
  • Opportunities
  • Awareness
  • Fun and accountability
  • Sense of achievement
  • Abilities
Research

Competitor Analysis

To identify gaps and opportunities for creating a more impactful sustainability app, I conducted a competitive analysis of existing platforms in the environmental awareness and green habits space. This analysis focused on the strengths and weaknesses of selected competitors, providing insights into areas for improvement in usability, engagement, and functionality.

Earth Hero

Earth Hero aims to empower users to make sustainable lifestyle choices through a wide range of eco-friendly activities and resources.

Pros:

  • Actionable Insights: Provides users with specific actions to reduce their carbon footprint.
  • Goal Tracking: Allows users to set and track sustainability goals, making it easy to visualize progress.

Cons:

  • Limited Community Engagement: Lacks a strong social or community feature, which could enhance accountability and shared motivation.
  • Minimal Gamification: Does not effectively use gamification to make sustainability efforts engaging or fun.

Ailuna

Ailuna encourages users to adopt sustainable habits through habit-building techniques and challenges.

Pros:

  • Habit-Forming Approach: Uses behavioral science principles to help users develop long-term sustainable habits.
  • User-Friendly Interface: Simple and intuitive interface makes navigation easy for users of all ages.

Cons:

  • Lack of Goal Customization: Limited flexibility in setting personalized goals, which may reduce user engagement.
  • No Achievement Rewards: Does not offer badges or rewards for achievements, missing an opportunity to motivate users.

JouleBug

JouleBug combines social interaction and gamification to encourage users to adopt environmentally friendly habits.

Pros:

  • Gamification: Uses points, badges, and challenges to make sustainability engaging and competitive.
  • Social Sharing: Allows users to share achievements with friends, fostering a sense of community.

Cons:

  • Overly Complex Navigation: The interface can be overwhelming for new users, with too many features cluttering the experience.
  • Limited Educational Resources: Focuses heavily on gamification, with fewer resources to educate users on the importance of each action.
Research

Affinity Mapping

Based on the insights gathered from desk research and competitor analysis, an affinity mapping session was conducted to organize and synthesize the key themes that emerged. This process aimed to identify actionable ideas that address user motivations, barriers, and gaps in existing solutions, ultimately guiding the design of a more effective sustainability app.

Research

Generating Insights

Through a detailed analysis of brainstormed ideas across themes like plantation, food and lifestyle, achievements, and metrics, I identified three core insights that could enhance user engagement in everyday environmental actions. These insights focus on collaboration, awareness, and a sense of accomplishment - each designed to make sustainability accessible, enjoyable, and rewarding.

Collaboration and Accountability for Motivation

Users can set individual or team goals with deadlines, fostering a sense of accountability and making the journey more enjoyable.

Awareness to Drive Informed Choices

Providing environmental knowledge through articles, stories, and accessible resources allows users to make informed lifestyle decisions.

Sense of Achievement as a Motivational Reward

Recognizing users' efforts through rewards, such as digital certificates, goodies, and offers, creates a sense of accomplishment.

Research

Defining the Problem

The complete understanding of the situation helped me frame the "how might we" statement to narrow down my problem statement in a way that fits a vast area of the underlying problem to provide an efficient solution. It focuses on making the process of making an impact more personal and fun.

How might we make people more aware of climate change issues through a digital platform so they make more sustainable life decisions in a fun yet accountable way.

User Persona

I developed this user persona to represent the primary user of the Sustain Dane Sustainability App, based on comprehensive research and user interviews. By synthesizing these insights, I aim to design a platform that empowers users to engage in sustainable actions, connect with a supportive community, and feel rewarded for their contributions toward environmental conservation.

Emma Green, 30

Marketing Specialist
Seattle, WA
I want to make a difference, but sometimes it feels overwhelming to know where to start.

Emma is an eco-conscious professional who wants to incorporate sustainability into her lifestyle but often feels uncertain about the impact of her actions. Although she actively recycles and uses eco-friendly products, she wishes to do more meaningful environmental activities that align with her daily routine. Emma values community and accountability, finding it easier to stick to goals when she collaborates with others. She wants a platform that guides her through sustainable choices and recognizes her efforts in a tangible way.

Goals

  • To incorporate small, impactful eco-friendly actions into her lifestyle.
  • To connect with a community of like-minded individuals who share sustainability goals.
  • To track her environmental impact and feel rewarded for her contributions.

Frustrations

  • Struggles to find reliable resources that simplify eco-friendly choices.
  • Feels isolated in her sustainability journey due to a lack of community support.
  • Lacks motivation when her sustainable actions don't feel impactful or acknowledged.

Motivations

  • Satisfaction: Knowing her actions contribute positively to the environment.
  • Community: Being part of a network of eco-conscious individuals.
  • Accountability: Setting and achieving sustainability goals with a partner.
  • Recognition: Receiving tangible rewards or acknowledgment for her efforts.

Needs

  • Guidance: Access to simple, actionable sustainability tips and resources.
  • Accountability Partners: Option to set goals with friends or colleagues to stay motivated.
  • Impact Visualization: A dashboard to track her contributions and see the difference she's making.
  • Recognition System: Badges or certificates to celebrate milestones and achievements.

This visualization provides valuable insights into Emma's journey, enabling a targeted design approach to support user motivation, engagement, and satisfaction in her pursuit of eco-friendly habits.

Ideation

Developing a Solution

The core idea of the design solution is to curate an application to encourage people to make sustainable and eco-friendly life choices through setting up individual or team goals and make the process of achieving these goals accountable and fun. This led to the inception of Sustain Dane Mobile App.

Ideation

Task Flows

From the theme insights generated after brainstorming, I created three user task flows that closely fulfill the idea of creating a sense of contribution, achievement, and awareness. The three task flows are:

Setting up individual sustainable goals

Setting up sustainable goals with a partner

Awarding users for their goal achievements

Ideation

Screen Designs

I sketched the first screen designs for each task flow screen. The goal was to produce flexible designs with room for changes and modifications later. The process also focussed on the completeness of the design by sketching all of the necessary elements on each screen.

Learnings

The entire journey of designing the UX for the Sustainability mobile app platform taught me to take a holistic approach to designing solutions to contribute to the climate crisis. From a design perspective, information architecture plays a very vital role in defining the entire design journey.
