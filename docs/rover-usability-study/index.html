Background

Understanding the company and its goals.

Rover.com is a platform designed to connect pet owners with trusted sitters and dog walkers, providing a seamless solution for pet care needs. Whether it's boarding, house sitting, or drop-in visits, Rover offers services tailored to busy pet owners who want the best care for their furry companions.

The platform features detailed sitter profiles, verified reviews, and secure booking options to ensure peace of mind. With millions of pet owners and sitters in its network, Rover.com has become a trusted name in the pet care industry, offering a user-friendly experience that prioritizes the safety, happiness, and well-being of pets.

Research

Research Goal

I conducted this usability study to evaluate the effectiveness of Rover.com, a platform designed to connect pet owners with reliable sitters and walkers. The goal of this research was to identify usability issues in key tasks such as navigating the homepage, searching for sitters, comparing profiles, and initiating the booking process. Insights from this study aim to inform design improvements that enhance the user experience, particularly for pet owners with specific needs.

The study involved three participants who completed tasks replicating real-world scenarios. Their feedback provided valuable insights into the platform's strengths and weaknesses.

Research

Research Questions

I chose the research questions to align with the overarching goal of evaluating the usability of Rover.com in searching and booking sitters and identifying areas for improvement. Each question addresses a critical component of the user journey, ensuring the study provides actionable insights into the platform's effectiveness and user experience.

How easily can users navigate and find information about booking a sitter on Rover.com?

This question aims to assess whether the homepage and site layout effectively guide users to important features, such as service options, filters, and sitter profiles. By understanding navigation barriers, this research can highlight areas where design enhancements could make the platform more intuitive and user-friendly, reducing frustration for first-time and returning users.

What challenges do users face when filtering, comparing, and selecting sitters?

Challenges in these areas can lead to time-consuming manual searches or user dissatisfaction. Addressing these issues is critical to improving user efficiency and satisfaction, as pet owners often have specific criteria such as experience, proximity, and cost. Insights from this question will guide improvements in search functionality and profile presentation.

How effective and intuitive is the booking process on Rover.com?

This question examines whether users can easily navigate through the booking workflow, understand pricing, and feel confident in completing their transactions. By analyzing this step, the research can uncover potential barriers and propose design changes that enhance trust and streamline the booking experience.

Recruitment

Recruitment Plan

To design and conduct the usability tests for Rover.com, I adopted a strategic recruitment approach to ensure the participation of representative users—pet owners with relevant experience and needs. The recruitment process involved careful planning to identify, target, and engage participants effectively, keeping in mind the study's goal of improving the platform's usability.

Recruitment

Screener Development

A screener survey was created using Google Forms to identify suitable participants. The form included targeted questions to confirm eligibility based on criteria such as pet ownership, experience with online service platforms, and comfort using digital devices. This ensured that participants were closely aligned with Rover.com's primary user base.The criteria included:

  • Owning at least one pet (cat or dog).
  • Experience with booking services online (e.g., food delivery, pet services, or travel).
  • Willingness to participate in a 45-minute usability session remotely.

Recruitment

Recruitment Method

The recruitment relied on the Interest Group method, focusing on online communities and organizations where pet owners naturally congregate. This approach allowed for direct engagement with individuals likely to use services like Rover.com. I targeted platforms such as pet owner groups on Reddit, pet care discussion forums, and local Facebook groups. These spaces provided access to users who were already invested in pet care solutions, making them ideal candidates for the study.

Usability Study

Overview

The study aims to evaluate Rover.com's effectiveness in providing a seamless and efficient user experience for pet owners navigating key functionalities; from initial exploration to booking a sitter, while identifying areas that impact decision-making and trust. Therefore, the usability study was conducted with intended tasks that could bring out potential challenges as well as the good aspects of the current design of the platform.

Usability Study

Tasks

The study aims to evaluate Rover.com's effectiveness in providing a seamless and efficient user experience for pet owners navigating key functionalities; from initial exploration to booking a sitter, while identifying areas that impact decision-making and trust. Therefore, the usability study was conducted with intended tasks that could bring out potential challenges as well as the good aspects of the current design of the platform.

  • Explore the Homepage: Participants navigated the homepage to gather initial impressions and familiarize themselves with the platform.
  • Search for a Sitter: Participants used filters and search options to find a sitter based on their preferences.
  • Compare and Select a Sitter: Participants reviewed sitter profiles to select a suitable candidate.
  • Simulate Booking: Participants initiated the booking process up to the payment stage.
Usability Study

Conducting the Study

The usability study for Rover.com was conducted in a controlled yet comfortable environment to replicate a natural browsing experience while ensuring accurate data collection. The study adhered to a structured process guided by a moderator, employing a combination of pre-test, in-test, and post-test questions to gather comprehensive insights.

Usability Study

Setup and Recording

Participants were invited to complete the study in a familiar setting using their own devices, which varied between laptops, tablets, and smartphones. This approach ensured that the tasks mirrored real-world usage scenarios. The sessions were audio-recorded with the participants’ consent to capture their feedback and think-aloud responses. Notes were also taken to supplement the recordings. For the pilot test, a living room setup was used, creating a relaxed environment conducive to natural interaction with the platform. While effective overall, minor adjustments were made to improve the recording quality and reduce background distractions.

Usability Study

Pre-Test Questions

Before starting the tasks, participants were asked questions to understand their prior experience and expectations:

  • “Have you ever booked a sitter or dog walker online before?”
  • “Which device do you usually use when making bookings online?”
  • “Can you tell me about the last time you booked a sitter or walker? What was that experience like?”
  • “Do you have any specific criteria when choosing a sitter or walker?”

These questions provided context for the participants' preferences and familiarity with similar platforms.

Usability Study

In-Test Questions

During the tasks, participants were encouraged to think aloud and reflect on their experiences. Prompts included:

  • “What stands out to you on this page?”
  • “What are you thinking as you complete this task?”
  • “Was there anything you found confusing or challenging?”
Usability Study

Post-Test Questions

After completing the tasks, participants were asked to reflect on their overall experience:

  • “What was your overall impression of using Rover.com today?”
  • “What was the best part of your experience?”
  • “What part of the experience did you find most frustrating?”
  • “Do you have any suggestions on how the booking process could be improved?”
  • “On a scale of 1-5, how easy did you find the process of booking a sitter or walker on Rover.com?”
Usability Study

Methods and Metrics

I selected these metrics because SEQ provides a simple yet effective way to gauge task difficulty. Time on Task and Error Rates quantify efficiency and potential pain points.

This structured process ensured that the usability study captured both quantitative data and qualitative insights, offering a comprehensive understanding of the user experience on Rover.com.

The study employed the following usability measures:

Single Ease Question (SEQ)

Participants rated the difficulty of each task on a scale of 1 to 5, providing a quantifiable measure of usability.

Time on Task

The duration taken to complete each task was recorded to assess efficiency.

Error Rates

Instances of confusion, missteps, or abandoned tasks were noted to identify usability challenges.

Usability Study

Results

The study revealed several recurring usability issues, detailed below, along with their severity, participant quotes, and illustrative examples.

Issue 1: Filtering Relevance

High Severity

Participants encountered irrelevant results when searching for sitters, such as profiles for dog sitters appearing in searches filtered for cats.

Participants Quotes

"I specifically searched for sitters with cat experience, but I still had to scroll through profiles with no mention of cats." - Participant A

"The filters worked, but I had to manually check if the sitter had experience with my specific pet type." - Participant C

Metrics

  • Time on Task: Participants spent an average of 5-7 minutes manually filtering through irrelevant profiles to find a suitable sitter, which exceeded the expected time of 1-2 minutes for this task.
  • Success Rate: 67% (Two out of three participants successfully found a relevant sitter, but only after extensive manual filtering.)

Design Implications

  • Improve filter accuracy to prioritize relevant results based on user-selected criteria.
  • Add a "species-specific" tag or checkbox for sitters to explicitly indicate experience with cats or dogs.

Issue 2: Lack of Cost Transparency

Medium Severity

The booking process did not provide an estimated total cost, including taxes and fees, before contacting the sitter.

Participants Quotes

"A price breakdown would help me decide faster without needing additional steps." - Participant A

"I wish the system gave me an idea of the total cost upfront instead of waiting until I reached out to the sitter." - Participant B

Metrics

  • Time on Task: Participants spent an average of 2 minutes on the booking process but expressed frustration over the absence of a total cost estimate, which delayed their decision-making.
  • Success Rate: 100% (All participants reached the booking stage but noted uncertainty about pricing due to missing cost breakdowns.)

Design Implications

  • Include a dynamic cost estimator that calculates the total cost based on selected dates and services.
  • Display a clear breakdown of fees on the booking page.

Issue 3: Service Descriptions Placement

Medium Severity

Information about services (e.g., boarding, drop-in visits) was located lower on the homepage, making it less accessible.

Participants Quotes

"It's great that the services are explained, but I wouldn't scroll down to see them if I were in a hurry." - Participant B

"Moving the descriptions up would make them more noticeable and helpful." - Participant C

Metrics

  • Time on Task: Participants spent an average of 30 seconds on the homepage, often missing the service descriptions placed lower on the page.
  • Success Rate: 33% (Only one out of three participants noticed the service descriptions during their initial homepage exploration.)

Design Implications

  • Relocate service descriptions to the top of the homepage.
  • Use concise tooltips or hover-over text to explain services in search filters.
