Initial Problem Discovery

After landing in Madison, WI, as an international student, the first thing to do was to order some groceries from a reputed store. After spending an hour on the Trader Joe's website, I came across the information that they do not sell products online. This cumbersome user experience left me pondering the potential for improvement. I thought there must surely be a solution to bridge this gap and enhance the grocery shopping experience for newcomers like myself.

Problem

A majority of potential shoppers meet a dead end on the Trader Joe's website while trying to checkout items. Other instore shoppers are not aware of the website and hence the user engagement on the website turns out to be quite low.

"Why is there a disconnect between Trader Joe's platform and other platforms in the same business domain"

Objective

The primary aim of our comprehensive research endeavor was driven by a commitment to understanding and addressing user pain-points on the Trader Joe's website with the overarching goal of enriching the online shopping journey and fostering greater satisfaction among customers.

White Paper Research

We studied the industrial trends and data and found that the trend of combining in-store and online grocery shopping is expected to continue due to the convenience it provides to customers. Also, in response to the post-pandemic grocery trends, enhancing the user experience through the website could be a potential approach. Below are few insights from McKinsey & Company reports:

  • According to McKinsey, online grocery spending is expected to increase to 7.2% in 2023, with a projected market penetration rate of 14–18% in the next 3–5 years.

  • 22% shoppers plan an in-store shopping trip combined with a prior online order, according to Nielsen IQ’s research.

Importance of online interface features:

  • Ease of use: product selection, saved preferences, and order history for reordering.

  • Self-serve access to information: stockouts, progress updates, and changes to orders.

Competitor Analysis

We started off with exploring and understanding two leading grocery firms, H-E-B and Kroger to find what factors impacted their success and how could we improve and reflect that in our own problem space.

Our findings revealed, although implementing delivery services might be challenging, a great amount of competitors demonstrate good examples of how they work and how they are benefited from optimizing the user experiences on their websites.
